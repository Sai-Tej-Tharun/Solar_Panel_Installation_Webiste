Service Overview

Getting solar right requires more than just buying panels. The wrong system size, poor technology choice, or missed subsidy opportunities can cost lakhs. Our consultation service ensures you enter the solar journey with complete knowledge and a clear plan.

Solar-Echos advisory covers the full spectrum — from a simple residential roof assessment and ROI calculation, to a full-scale industrial energy audit with Detailed Project Report, RESCO/PPA contract advisory, and SERC open access guidance. Our team includes MNRE-certified energy auditors, licensed electrical engineers, and financial analysts with deep solar experience.

Our advisory is deliberately vendor-neutral for analysis and design phases — we recommend the best technology for your needs, not the brand we have the highest margin on. If you choose Solar-Echos for installation, great. If you prefer another EPC contractor after our advisory, that's fine too — our job is to make sure you get the best outcome.

Unbiased — we work for you, not for panel brands

Our consultants are paid on a fixed-fee basis, not on commission from equipment sales. That means our technology recommendations are based purely on what's best for your site, budget, and goals.

Our Consultation Process

1

Initial Discovery Call (Free)

A 30-minute call with a senior consultant to understand your goals, property type, energy spend, timeline, and budget. We assess whether your project needs a residential, commercial, or industrial consultation package and give you a clear brief of deliverables.

30 minutes — Free of charge
2

Data Collection & Site Visit

We collect 12 months of electricity bills, load profile data, roof or land area photos and measurements, and GPS coordinates for solar irradiance modelling. For commercial projects, we conduct an in-person energy audit of your facility.

Day 1–3
3

Analysis & Modelling

Using PVSyst or HOMER Pro, we model your site's solar resource, system layout, annual energy yield, and grid interaction. We prepare a comprehensive financial model with 25-year cash flows, IRR, NPV, LCOE, and scenario analysis for conservative, base, and optimistic cases.

Day 3–7
4

Advisory Report Delivery

You receive a professionally formatted report covering: executive summary, site assessment, recommended system configuration, financial analysis, subsidy eligibility, regulatory roadmap, suggested EPC shortlist with evaluation criteria, and risk factors. A video walkthrough session is included.

Day 7–10
5

DPR Preparation (if required)

For commercial and industrial projects requiring bank financing or DISCOM approval, we prepare a full Detailed Project Report including single-line diagram, structural calculations, P&L projections, and all annexures required by lenders and regulators.

+5–7 days
6

Implementation Support

Once you select your EPC contractor, we optionally provide project management oversight — reviewing contractor designs, approving equipment procurement, and inspecting installation quality against the advisory report specifications. This ensures your project is executed exactly as planned.

Through project completion

A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is a comprehensive technical and financial document required for: (1) applying for a solar loan from a bank or IREDA, (2) applying for DISCOM net-metering or group captive approval for systems above 500 kW, and (3) availing of some state-level subsidies. It includes single-line diagrams, structural calculations, equipment specifications, site photos, yield estimates, and financial projections. Our DPRs are accepted by all major lenders.
Under a RESCO (Renewable Energy Service Company) or PPA (Power Purchase Agreement) model, a developer installs solar on your property at no upfront cost and sells you the electricity at a fixed, below-market rate for 15–25 years. You get clean energy savings from Day 1 without investing capital. Our advisory helps you understand if this model suits your situation and how to negotiate a fair PPA tariff and exit provisions.
A free quote from an installer is optimised to close a sale — it will almost always recommend the installer's preferred equipment at their preferred margin. Our advisory is paid on a fixed-fee basis and covers the entire market. We build financial models using real irradiance data (not optimistic assumptions), identify subsidy opportunities the installer may not mention, and objectively compare multiple technology options. The difference typically saves clients 15–25% on total project cost.
