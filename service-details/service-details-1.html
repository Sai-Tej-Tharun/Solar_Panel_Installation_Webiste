Our Installation Process

1

Free Site Survey & Assessment

Our certified engineer visits your property to assess roof orientation, structural strength, shading patterns, electricity consumption, and local DISCOM net-metering policies. A detailed feasibility report is shared within 24 hours.

Day 1 — Free of charge
2

Custom System Design & Proposal

Using site data and shade analysis software, we create a customised system design — panel layout, inverter sizing, mounting structure, cable routing, and projected energy yield. You receive a transparent, itemised proposal with ROI projections.

Day 2–3
3

Permits, Subsidy & DISCOM Filing

We apply for all required permits — structural NOC, electrical safety certificate, DISCOM net-metering application, and PM Surya Ghar subsidy registration. Our team tracks every approval and keeps you updated in real time.

Day 3–7 (authority-dependent)
4

Equipment Procurement

Tier-1 monocrystalline solar panels, a string or micro-inverter, mounting structures, AC/DC isolators, bidirectional meter, cables, and our IoT monitoring module are procured from certified suppliers and delivered to your site.

Day 5–8
5

Professional Installation

Our two-person certified crew installs the complete system in 1–2 days: rooftop mounting structure, panel fixing, inverter wiring, earthing, and main switchboard connection. The site is left clean; your daily routine is not disrupted.

1–2 days on-site
6

Commissioning & Handover

We power up the system, test all safety interlocks, verify net-meter synchronisation, and set up your monitoring dashboard. You receive a full handover kit: warranty cards, system manual, app login, and emergency contact details.

Day 9–10 — You're live!

Frequently Asked Questions

A 5 kW solar system typically requires approximately 400–500 sq ft of shadow-free roof space, assuming standard 400W monocrystalline panels (each requiring about 20 sq ft). The actual space depends on panel efficiency, tilt angle, and any obstructions like water tanks or staircases. Our engineer will determine the optimal layout during the free site survey.
Standard grid-tied (on-grid) systems automatically shut down during power cuts as a safety measure. If backup power is important to you, we recommend a hybrid system that includes a battery. Our Standard and Premium plans include a hybrid-inverter that accepts battery storage. Batteries can be added later as well.
With net metering, excess units are exported to the DISCOM grid and credited to your account at the applicable feed-in tariff (₹2–4/unit depending on state). These credits are then offset against your night-time or cloudy-day consumption. At the end of each billing cycle, you pay only the net difference — often resulting in a near-zero bill.
Solar panels have no moving parts and require minimal maintenance. The main task is cleaning — dust, bird droppings, and pollen can reduce output by 10–20% if left unattended. We recommend cleaning every 2–3 months. Our AMC plans cover quarterly inspections, panel cleaning, inverter health checks, and remote monitoring alerts so you never have to worry.
Most RCC rooftops in India can comfortably support solar panels (~12 kg/m²). Our site survey includes a basic structural assessment. If required, we can recommend a structural engineer for a formal certification. For sheet-metal or older tiled roofs, lightweight mounting structures are available. Very few rooftops actually fail the structural check.
We work exclusively with Tier-1 BIS-approved panels: Tata Power Solar, Vikram Solar, Waaree, Longi, and Adani Solar. All are MNRE-listed modules. For inverters we partner with Sungrow, SolarEdge, Havells, and Growatt. Your proposal will specify the exact brand and model based on your site requirements and budget.
