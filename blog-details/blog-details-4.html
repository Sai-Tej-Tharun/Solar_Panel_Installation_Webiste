Understanding Panel Technologies

Three technologies dominate the 2025 Indian solar market, each with meaningfully different efficiency profiles, costs, and suitability for Indian conditions.

Monocrystalline PERC panels are the most widely installed type in India. Cut from a single silicon crystal, they offer efficiencies of 19–22% and have a proven 15-year track record. They perform well in direct sunlight but lose output faster than other technologies in high heat.

Bifacial panels generate electricity from both front and rear surfaces, capturing reflected light (albedo) from the roof surface below. In Indian conditions, the rear-side gain is typically 5–12% — lower than in open-ground or carport installations where albedo is higher. They are most valuable on white-painted rooftops or metal sheet roofs.

TOPCon (Tunnel Oxide Passivated Contact) is the newest commercially available technology, offering efficiencies of 22–24.5% with the industry's lowest degradation rates (0.4% per year). The per-watt cost premium over PERC has narrowed to about 8–12% in 2025, making TOPCon the best value choice for most premium residential installations.

What to Look For: Key Metrics

The 6 Numbers That Matter
  • Efficiency (%): Higher = more power per square foot. Critical for space-limited rooftops.
  • Temperature Coefficient (%/°C): Lower (less negative) = less output loss in heat. Critical in India where panel temps reach 60–75°C.
  • Annual Degradation Rate (%): Lower = more power and money over 25 years. Even 0.1% difference costs you thousands.
  • Power Tolerance: Always choose panels with a positive power tolerance (e.g. 0/+5W), meaning you always get at least rated power.
  • BIS Certification: Mandatory for PM Surya Ghar subsidy eligibility. Always verify.
  • Warranty: Minimum 10-year product warranty + 25-year linear performance guarantee at ≥80% output.

Indian Brands vs Imported: The Honest Truth

Indian manufacturers — Adani Solar, Waaree, Vikram Solar, Goldi Solar — have made enormous strides in quality over the past five years. Their panels are BIS certified, competitively priced, and come with strong domestic service networks. For budget-conscious buyers, they represent excellent value.

Where Chinese Tier-1 brands (Longi, JA Solar, Trina, Canadian Solar) still hold a meaningful advantage is in cell-level technology. Their TOPCon and HJT manufacturing lines are 12–24 months ahead of Indian manufacturers' equivalent product launches. If you are optimising for maximum lifetime output per square foot, the Chinese TOPCon brands currently lead.

Note on Customs Duties

India imposes a 25% Basic Customs Duty (BCD) on imported solar modules. Despite this, Chinese TOPCon panels remain competitively priced because of the scale advantages in manufacturing. BCD is expected to be maintained until domestic manufacturing catches up, likely 2027–2028.

Temperature Performance in Indian Heat

This is the most under-discussed aspect of Indian solar panel selection. When a 545W panel rated at STC (25°C cell temperature) is installed on a Pune rooftop in May, the panel temperature easily reaches 68°C. At that temperature, a panel with a temperature coefficient of -0.35%/°C loses approximately 15% of its rated output.

The REC Alpha's best-in-class coefficient of -0.24%/°C means it loses only about 10% in the same conditions — generating meaningfully more power on your hottest, sunniest days. Over a full Indian summer, this difference compounds significantly.

Our Recommendation by Use Case

Best Value for Most Indian Homes

JA Solar JAM72D40 (TOPCon Bifacial) or Trina Vertex S+. Both deliver excellent TOPCon efficiency at a reasonable price premium over PERC. The bifacial version adds 5–10% extra yield on reflective rooftops. Ideal for 3–10 kW residential systems.

Best Performance, No Compromise

REC Alpha Pure Black. The best temperature coefficient in the market (-0.24%/°C) and the lowest degradation (0.25%/yr). Unmatched for hot Indian climates. Premium price of ₹48/W justified by 25-year product warranty and superior heat performance.

Best Budget Choice (Indian Brand)

Adani Solar 575W Mono PERC. Best-value Indian panel with BIS certification, domestic service network, and a respectable 10-year product warranty. At ₹28/W it is 40% cheaper than the REC Alpha while still delivering solid 21.1% efficiency.
