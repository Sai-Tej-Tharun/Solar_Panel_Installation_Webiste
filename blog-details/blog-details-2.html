Why Simple Payback Fails

The most common way people evaluate a solar investment is the simple payback period: divide the total system cost by the annual electricity savings. Install a 5 kW system for ₹3,00,000, save ₹72,000 per year, and you get a payback of 4.2 years. Simple. Intuitive. And dangerously incomplete.

Simple payback ignores three things that matter enormously over a 25-year asset: the time value of money, annual electricity tariff inflation, and gradual panel output degradation. When you ignore these, you may underestimate your true returns by a factor of two or more — or overestimate them on a poorly designed system.

"Solar is not a consumer purchase — it is a capital investment. It should be evaluated with the same rigour you'd apply to a fixed deposit, a rental property, or an equity SIP."

— Pooja Rao, Solar Echos Financial Analyst

The 5 Inputs You Need

Before you can build a proper model, you need five numbers. Each one has a reasonable default if you don't have exact data.

The two highlighted inputs are the ones most people forget. Electricity tariffs in India have risen at an average of 5.8% per year over the last decade. A system that saves you ₹72,000 today will save you ₹1,21,000 in year 10 — in nominal terms — even with zero change in your consumption pattern.

NPV and IRR Explained Simply

Net Present Value (NPV) answers the question: if I could earn 7% on my money elsewhere (say, in a fixed deposit), is this solar investment still worth more? If NPV is positive, solar beats your next-best alternative. For a well-designed Indian rooftop system, NPV is almost always strongly positive at a 7% discount rate.

Internal Rate of Return (IRR) gives you the annualised return percentage of the investment, accounting for all cash flows over the full 25 years. For most residential systems in India, the IRR falls between 8% and 14% — comfortably beating fixed deposits, debt mutual funds, and matching or exceeding many equity instruments on a risk-adjusted basis.

Key Insight

A solar system with a 12% IRR is the risk-free equivalent of a 12% fixed return — because your savings are guaranteed by physics, not market conditions. No equity investment offers that certainty.

The Electricity Inflation Effect

This is the single most underappreciated factor in solar economics. When tariff inflation is 6% per year, the value of every unit your solar panels generate increases by 6% each year. Your panels don't know this — they just keep generating the same electrons. But the rupee value of those electrons grows every year, compounding in your favour.

Modelling a conservative 5% tariff inflation versus an optimistic 8% changes the 25-year NPV by over ₹4 lakh on a typical 5 kW system. Always run your model at both ends to understand the range of outcomes.

Accounting for Panel Degradation

All solar panels lose output over time. The industry standard warranty guarantees that a good monocrystalline panel retains at least 80% of its rated output after 25 years — implying a maximum degradation rate of about 0.87% per year. Premium bifacial TOPCon panels typically degrade at 0.4–0.5% per year.

The difference between a 0.5% and 0.8% annual degradation rate translates to roughly ₹1.8 lakh in lifetime savings difference on a 5 kW system. It is one of the clearest justifications for paying a premium for Tier-1 panels.

Three Real-World Examples

Example 1: 3 kW Home System — Pune, Maharashtra

Net cost after subsidy: ₹87,000 · Annual savings (Year 1): ₹38,250 · Simple payback: 2.3 years · 25-year NPV at 7%: ₹4,10,000 · IRR: 18.4%

Example 2: 10 kW Commercial System — Ahmedabad, Gujarat

Net cost after subsidy: ₹3,72,000 · Annual savings (Year 1): ₹1,43,000 · Simple payback: 2.6 years · 25-year NPV at 7%: ₹16,20,000 · IRR: 17.1%

Example 3: 5 kW Clinic — Jaipur, Rajasthan

Net cost after subsidy: ₹1,97,000 · Annual savings (Year 1): ₹78,200 · Simple payback: 2.5 years · 25-year NPV at 7%: ₹9,10,000 · IRR: 19.2%

Final Verdict

Indian rooftop solar is, on a risk-adjusted basis, one of the best investments available to the average homeowner or small business today. IRRs of 12–19% with zero market risk, backed by a 25-year product warranty and a government subsidy that halves your upfront cost — no other asset class comes close.

The simple payback period tells you when you break even. The full 25-year model tells you how wealthy you become. Always run both — but make your decision based on the latter.
