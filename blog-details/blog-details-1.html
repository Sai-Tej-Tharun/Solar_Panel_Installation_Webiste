What is PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana?

Launched in February 2024 by Prime Minister Narendra Modi, the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana (Free Electricity Scheme) is India's largest-ever rooftop solar programme. With a budget of ₹75,021 crore, it aims to install rooftop solar systems on 1 crore households across India by 2027 — and in doing so, provide each qualifying home with up to 300 units of free electricity every month.

The scheme is administered by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) and implemented through Distribution Companies (DISCOMs) across all 28 states and 8 union territories. Unlike older MNRE rooftop programmes, PM Surya Ghar disburses the subsidy directly to the homeowner's bank account after installation — a key improvement that eliminates the middleman and reduces fraud.

Key Highlight

Under PM Surya Ghar, a household installing a 3 kW system can receive a central subsidy of ₹78,000 credited directly to their bank account — equivalent to roughly 50% of the total system cost — making it the most generous solar subsidy India has ever offered to residential consumers.

The scheme also works in conjunction with nationalised and private banks to offer collateral-free loans at concessional interest rates, so homeowners who cannot afford the upfront cost (even after subsidy) can still participate.

Subsidy Amounts — How Much Can You Get?

The central government subsidy is calculated as a flat amount per kilowatt of installed capacity, with a higher per-kW rate for the first 2 kW and a reduced rate for the third kilowatt. Systems above 3 kW receive no additional central subsidy (though several states top this up — see Section 6).

System Size Central Subsidy % of Avg. Cost Est. Monthly Savings
1 kW ₹30,000 60% ₹800 – ₹1,200
2 kW ₹60,000 55% ₹1,600 – ₹2,400
3 kW ₹78,000 50% ₹2,400 – ₹3,600
Above 3 kW ₹78,000 (cap) 30–40% ₹3,600+

The subsidy is disbursed in two tranches: 50% after installation is confirmed by the DISCOM inspector, and 50% after the net meter is commissioned and your first export reading is recorded. Most homeowners receive the full amount within 60–90 days of installation.

Eligibility Criteria

The scheme is open to all residential consumers across India, but there are a few important conditions to check before you apply.

Eligibility Checklist
  • Ownership: You must own the house (or have landlord's written permission for rented premises)
  • Electricity connection: A valid, single-phase or three-phase residential electricity connection in your name
  • Roof type: RCC, metal sheet, or any structurally sound rooftop — minimum 10 sq ft per kW (100 sq ft for 1 kW)
  • No prior subsidy: You must not have claimed a solar subsidy from MNRE under any previous scheme
  • DISCOM empanelment: The vendor you hire must be empanelled with your state's DISCOM
  • Bank account: Active bank account linked to Aadhaar for direct benefit transfer

Note that housing societies and apartment complexes can also apply for rooftop solar on common areas (such as terrace, carport, or clubhouse rooftop) under the scheme, though the process involves a slightly different set of approvals from the Resident Welfare Association (RWA).

Step-by-Step Application Process

The entire process is online, through the National Portal for Rooftop Solar at pmsuryaghar.gov.in. Here's exactly what you need to do, in order.

  1. 1

    Register on the portal

    Visit pmsuryaghar.gov.in and click "Apply for Rooftop Solar." Register using your state, DISCOM name, electricity consumer number, and mobile number. An OTP verification links your account to your electricity connection.

  2. 2

    Submit rooftop solar application

    Fill in your rooftop details, upload a recent electricity bill, and submit your application. The DISCOM will conduct a feasibility check within 30 days and confirm the maximum system size your connection can support (usually your sanctioned load divided by 0.8).

  3. 3

    Choose an empanelled vendor

    The portal provides a list of vendors empanelled with your DISCOM, along with their ratings and price per watt. Compare at least 3 vendors. Do not hire an un-empanelled vendor — you will not receive the subsidy even if the installation is completed.

  4. 4

    Installation and inspection

    Once you sign the contract, your vendor installs the system (typically within 5–7 working days). The DISCOM then schedules an inspection within 15 days of your commissioning request to verify the installation against the sanctioned specifications.

  5. 5

    Net meter commissioning

    After inspection approval, the DISCOM replaces your old meter with a bidirectional net meter within 30 days. From this date, your system begins exporting surplus power back to the grid, earning you credits on your electricity bill.

  6. 6

    Subsidy disbursement

    Submit your bank account details and a cancelled cheque on the portal. The first tranche (50%) is credited within 30 days of DISCOM inspection approval. The second tranche follows within 30 days of net meter commissioning.

DISCOM Approval & Net Metering

The DISCOM approval process is the step where most applications experience delays. Understanding the timelines — and your rights if they are breached — can save you weeks of waiting.

Under the MNRE guidelines, DISCOMs are legally required to complete feasibility checks within 15 working days and net meter installation within 30 working days of a commissioning application. If your DISCOM misses these deadlines, you can raise a grievance on the National Portal, which escalates to the state electricity regulator.

Watch Out

Several DISCOMs are currently facing a backlog due to the surge in applications. In Maharashtra, Rajasthan, and Gujarat, average net meter wait times have stretched to 45–60 days as of July 2025. Factor this into your timeline expectations — but do not let it deter you from applying. The subsidy is worth the wait.

Net metering means your electricity meter can measure both import (grid power you consume) and export (solar power you send back). At the end of each billing cycle, only the net difference is billed or credited. If you export more than you import, the surplus is carried forward as a credit (in most states, for up to 12 months).

If you are in Gujarat and install a 3 kW system, you could receive the ₹78,000 central subsidy plus up to ₹60,000 from Gujarat's additional scheme — bringing your total subsidy to ₹1,38,000. At that level, the payback period for a typical 3 kW system is under 18 months.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Based on thousands of applications processed through Solar Echos, here are the seven mistakes we see most often — and how to avoid each one.

  1. Hiring an un-empanelled vendor. Always verify your vendor's name on the DISCOM empanelment list before signing anything. Re-checking on the portal on the day you sign is a good habit, as lists are updated monthly.
  2. Oversizing the system. You can only claim subsidy up to your DISCOM-approved system size. A vendor who installs more than the sanctioned capacity without prior approval puts your entire subsidy at risk.
  3. Not registering on the portal first. Some homeowners let vendors "handle the paperwork," but the portal registration must be in your name and linked to your Aadhaar. Verify every form before submission.
  4. Using non-BIS-certified panels. All panels installed under the scheme must comply with BIS standards. Check the BIS registration number on your panels before installation begins.
  5. Missing the 60-day commissioning window. After DISCOM feasibility approval, you have 60 days to complete installation and apply for inspection. Missing this deadline means restarting the process.
  6. Incorrect bank details. The direct benefit transfer fails if your bank account number or IFSC code is entered incorrectly. Double-check both on the portal before submitting.
  7. Not getting a commissioning certificate. Always collect the official commissioning certificate from your DISCOM after net meter installation. This is required for warranty claims and future system upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply if I live in an apartment?

Yes, apartment dwellers can apply for solar on common rooftop areas through their RWA. Individual flat owners can also apply if they have independent roof access (typically in builder floors or ground-floor flats with terrace rights). Check with your DISCOM for the specific process in your city.

Is the ₹78,000 subsidy in addition to the 30% tax benefit?

The PM Surya Ghar subsidy is separate from income tax benefits. If you are a self-employed individual or business, you can additionally claim depreciation benefits under Section 32 of the Income Tax Act. Salaried employees are not eligible for the depreciation benefit but can still fully claim the PM Surya Ghar subsidy.

What if I sell my house after installing solar?

The solar system and any remaining warranty are transferable to the new owner upon sale. The DISCOM will need to update the electricity consumer account, and the new owner will assume the net metering arrangement. The subsidy received by you is yours to keep — there is no clawback provision for property sales.

How do I track my application status?

Log in to pmsuryaghar.gov.in and navigate to "Track Application Status." Each stage — feasibility check, inspection, net metering, subsidy disbursement — has a timestamp. You can also call the MNRE helpline at +91 (987) 654-3210 (toll-free) for escalations.
